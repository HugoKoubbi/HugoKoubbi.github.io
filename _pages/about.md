I am a PhD student in mathematics at [CEREMADE](https://www.ceremade.dauphine.fr/) interested in the mathematical analysis of neural networks and modern learning systems. My work sits at the interface of analysis, optimization, probability, and machine learning.

My current interests include optimization dynamics, self-attention and transformers, metastability, and tools from nonlinear PDEs, optimal transport, and the calculus of variations. A current anchor for this work is the doctoral project [Slow Motion in Machine Learning Landscapes](https://theses.fr/s419119).

This website is based on the [al-folio](https://github.com/alshedivat/al-folio) template and is meant to stay simple: a short bio, a publication list, and a few links. You can also find me on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=rp1SemcAAAAJ&hl=en) and [OpenReview](https://openreview.net/profile?id=~Hugo_Koubbi1).
